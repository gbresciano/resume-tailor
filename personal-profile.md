## What You Are NOT

These framings should never appear in a resume, even if a JD seems to invite them:

- A pure visual designer
- A pure software engineer
- A media industry expert
- A deep AI/ML researcher

---

## Resume Title — Fixed Set

The resume title is **not** a direct mirror of the JD's role title. Pick from this fixed set:

- **For PM roles**, pick the seniority variant that matches the JD level: "Senior Product Manager," "Lead Product Manager," "Staff Product Manager," "Principal Product Manager," "Group Product Manager."
- **For director-level, VP-level, or Head of Product roles**, use **"Product Leader."**
- **For adjacent or non-PM roles** (Product Engineer, Product Designer, Founding Engineer, etc.), use **"Product Leader."**

---

## AI Scope — Conservative Register

AI work has been real but limited in scope: third-party LLM API integrations, resume parsing, AI-assisted content generation. Phrasings must reflect that level without overstating.

**Examples at the right level** (illustrative, not closed — anything at the same register is fine):

- "AI-assisted workflows"
- "LLM integrations"
- "Integrated third-party AI APIs"
- "Shipped AI capabilities into consumer products"
- "AI-powered features"
- "LLM-powered [specific feature]"

**Never use phrasings that imply deeper AI/ML expertise:**

- "AI-native product leader"
- "Advanced ML systems"
- "AI-first platform"
- "Deep AI expertise"
- "Built an AI engine"
- "ML model development"

**Mirror JD AI keywords when truthful.** If the JD says "LLM integrations" or "AI-powered features," use that phrase verbatim. If the JD uses heavier framing ("AI-first," "ML systems") that actual experience doesn't support, **do not mirror it** — keep the conservative register above.

## Cross-Cutting Accuracy Constraints

These are truth claims that span multiple roles and override JD demands, even at the cost of ATS match score.

1. **No AI inflation.** See the conservative register above.
2. **No video editing claims.** Crikle work was video player UX and real-time communication only — not full editing timelines or post-production workflows.
3. **No inflated people management.** Only reference team leadership where the JD requires it and the experience genuinely supports it.
