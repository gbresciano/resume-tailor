# Tailoring Playbook

This file guides Claude (and Guillo) on how to adapt the master resume for
different role types. Reference this alongside the master resume when generating
any tailored version.

---

## How To Read a Job Description

When analysing a JD, look for:

1. **Role level signal** — IC vs. lead, individual contributor vs. manager
2. **Domain signal** — consumer vs. B2B, SaaS vs. marketplace, mobile vs. web
3. **Product maturity signal** — 0→1 vs. scaling vs. optimising
4. **Tech environment signal** — startup vs. scaleup vs. enterprise
5. **Differentiator signal** — what is the JD most excited about?
   (e.g. "AI-native", "growth mindset", "design sensibility", "data-driven")

Map these signals to the best positioning angle from the master resume before
writing a single word of the resume.

## Positioning Angles — When To Use Each

### 1. Senior / Principal Product Manager
**Use when:** The JD is for an IC PM role at a growth-stage or scaleup company.

**Lead with:** Goodnotes (scale + consumer + mobile + subscription) → Loop (activation + metrics) → Folum or Travellar for product breadth.

**Summary tone:** Data-informed, strategic, product-led, consumer-experienced.

**Sample title line:**
`Senior Product Manager | Consumer SaaS · Engagement & Growth · Mobile-First Products`

**Bullets to prioritise:**
- Goodnotes: subscription switch, MAU growth, revenue doubling, AI features
- Loop: activation improvements (8% → 17%), MAU and paying user growth
- Crikle: roadmap ownership, delivery, cross-functional leadership

**De-emphasise:** Engineering implementation details, founder/maker framing.

---

### 2. Head of Product / Product Lead
**Use when:** The JD implies strategic ownership, team leadership, or VP-adjacent scope.

**Lead with:** Loop and Crikle (Head of Product titles) → Goodnotes (scale credibility) → Folum (founding context).

**Summary tone:** Strategic, cross-functional, experienced leading product direction.

**Sample title line:**
`Head of Product | Product Strategy · Cross-Platform · Consumer & Collaboration`

**Bullets to prioritise:**
- Loop: +18% MAU, +24% paying users, activation doubled, cross-platform roadmap
- Crikle: product strategy, engineering leadership, video UX
- Goodnotes: owned consumer product for Apple platforms, 28M MAU

**De-emphasise:** Day-to-day engineering implementation, solo builder framing.

---

### 3. Product Engineer / Founding Product Engineer
**Use when:** The JD asks for someone who can both build and define product.

**Lead with:** Folum (full-stack, 0→1, GCP/Node/React/TypeScript) → Travellar (same stack, social product) → Goodnotes (PM credibility).

**Summary tone:** Builder-oriented, hands-on, technical, product-minded.

**Sample title line:**
`Founding Product Engineer | Full-Stack · TypeScript · React · Product Architecture`

**Bullets to prioritise:**
- Folum: modular block architecture, full-stack implementation, 0→1, LLM integration
- Travellar: offline-first, social product, end-to-end ownership, rapid iteration
- Crikle: Twilio Video, real-time communication, Node.js, React.js

**De-emphasise:** Team leadership, PM process, stakeholder management.

---

### 4. Product Designer / Founding Product Designer
**Use when:** The JD values design systems, interaction design, and UX flows.
*(Use this angle carefully — only position as designer if the JD truly fits.)*

**Lead with:** Folum (UX design, interaction design, component systems) → Travellar (UX flows, discovery, social UX) → Goodnotes (UX craft, Apple-platform UX).

**Summary tone:** UX-informed, systems thinker, prototypes in code.

**Sample title line:**
`Product Designer & Builder | Interaction Design · UX Systems · Prototyping in Code`

**Bullets to prioritise:**
- Folum: UX design, modular layout systems, component architecture
- Travellar: discovery flows, activity creation UX, engagement loops
- Goodnotes: UX flows, onboarding, Apple platform UX

**De-emphasise:** Engineering depth, PM process, data/analytics framing.

---

### 5. Consumer SaaS PM
**Use when:** The JD mentions engagement, retention, subscription, growth, mobile, or consumer product.

**Lead with:** Goodnotes (consumer scale + subscription + mobile) → Loop (activation + growth metrics) → Travellar (engagement + social).

**Summary tone:** Consumer-experienced, metric-driven, product-growth focused.

**Sample title line:**
`Senior Product Manager | Consumer SaaS · Subscription Growth · Mobile-First Experiences`

**Bullets to prioritise:**
- Goodnotes: all major metrics, subscription model transition, AI features
- Loop: activation and paying user metrics
- Folum: subscription monetisation, user feedback loops

---

### 6. Collaboration / Productivity Platform PM
**Use when:** The JD is for a notes app, workspace tool, communication platform, or productivity SaaS.

**Lead with:** Goodnotes (productivity at scale) → Loop (collaboration hub) → Crikle (video/comms).

**Summary tone:** Collaboration-minded, cross-platform, onboarding and retention experienced.

**Sample title line:**
`Senior Product Manager | Productivity · Collaboration · Cross-Platform Consumer Products`

**Bullets to prioritise:**
- Goodnotes: productivity app, Apple platform, subscription, 28M MAU
- Loop: collaboration hub, cross-platform, onboarding, activation
- Crikle: video communication, real-time workflows

---

### 7. AI Workflow / AI-Assisted Product PM
**Use when:** The JD mentions AI tools, LLM integration, AI-native products, or AI workflow design.

**Lead with:** Goodnotes (LLM-powered AI features at scale) → Folum (LLM resume parsing) → Travellar (AI-assisted content generation).

**Summary tone:** Pragmatic AI PM — experienced shipping AI features, grounded in user value.

**Sample title line:**
`Senior Product Manager | AI-Assisted Workflows · Consumer SaaS · LLM Integration`

**Critical constraint:** Never claim "AI-native" or "deep ML expertise". Use:
- "AI-assisted workflows"
- "LLM-powered features"
- "Integrating third-party AI APIs"
- "Shipped AI capabilities into consumer products"

## Fit Assessment Guide

Use this to quickly assess before tailoring:

| Signal in JD | Fit Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Consumer SaaS, mobile, subscription | Strong | Goodnotes is a perfect anchor |
| Collaboration / productivity tools | Strong | Goodnotes + Loop + Crikle all apply |
| 0→1 / founding / builder role | Strong | Folum + Travellar well-suited |
| AI-assisted product / LLM workflows | Strong | Honest framing available |
| Video/comms products | Strong | Crikle directly relevant |
| Enterprise SaaS / B2B | Medium | Reframe Loop/Crikle for B2B context |
| Pure design role | Medium | Position carefully; UX credible, not visual |
| Fintech / Healthcare domain | Medium | Cross-functional skills transfer; no domain depth |
| Deep ML / AI research PM | Weak | Avoid; framing would require overstatement |
| Staff/Principal Engineering | Weak | Coding experience exists but not at that seniority |
| Pure visual design | Weak | Decline or reframe heavily |

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Starting bullets with "Responsible for..." — rewrite as active verb
- Using "Worked closely with..." — rewrite as what was achieved
- Vague bullets like "Improved user experience" — add specifics or metrics
- Overloading the summary with adjectives rather than substance
- Listing skills that aren't evidenced in the experience section
- Using the same resume structure for a PM role vs. a founding engineer role
